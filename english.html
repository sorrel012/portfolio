Period 03.28.2023 - 04.07.2023
Team members Four people
DB ORACLE 11g Enterprise
IDE SQL Developer
Description

It is a system that manages various functions required for the operation of an education center. The primary users include administrators, instructors, and students, who have different functionalities available based on their access permissions.

First, we organized the entities, relationships, and attributes necessary for the operation of an education center through conceptual modeling. Based on the organized entities, relationships, and attributes, we proceeded with logical data modeling. After going through the normalization process, we designed the logical data model and finally implemented a relational database through physical modeling for the operation of the education management system.

üßæ Feedback

The key aspect of this project was the ERD (Entity-Relationship Diagram) model. During the initial project meeting, the team thoroughly performed the modeling process, and we discussed writing the business queries based on the model. Through multiple normalizations, we separated tables to avoid data duplication. This provided a solid foundation for defining data smoothly.

However, we encountered challenges when manipulating data. Although we went through the normalization process, we overlooked the importance of proper denormalization. During normalization, we believed that manipulating data through joins would be easier and proceeded with physical modeling. While the original plan allowed data manipulation through joins, as we started to join multiple tables, the readability decreased, and it became difficult to understand the business logic by looking at the queries. Although creating views improved performance and readability to some extent, integrating the tables properly before that would have been more effective. I realized the importance of not only normalization but also appropriate denormalization to enhance efficiency when designing a database
